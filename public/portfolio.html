01 — about

Who I am

An engineer who builds things that matter — at the intersection of signal, silicon, and software.

I'm Pabasara Ilankoon — an Electronic & Telecommunication Engineering undergraduate at General Sir John Kotelawala Defence University, with a parallel BSc. in Information Technology from UCSC.

My work lives at the boundary between hardware and intelligence: ESP32 and Raspberry Pi edge nodes, YOLOv8 models compressed to ONNX, LoRa meshes that work when everything else fails, and Flutter apps that bring it all to a screen someone can actually use.

What drives me is applying engineering to real problems in Sri Lanka — railway safety, disaster response, agriculture, and mine monitoring. I believe the best embedded systems are invisible: they just work, reliably, in the field, with no internet required.

04 — research

Research

Academic work with real deployments and a preprint manuscript under preparation.

Preprint 2024 – Present

Elevision: A Real-Time AI-Powered Elephant Detection and Alert System for Railway Safety in Sri Lanka

Researching and implementing an edge AI deployment for human-elephant conflict prevention on Sri Lankan railways. The system combines a custom-trained YOLOv8 model compressed to ONNX for Raspberry Pi 4 inference, Firebase cloud services, a GSM fallback alerting chain, and Flutter + React.js monitoring interfaces. Prototype node RW-001 is deployed and operational at Palugaswewa Railway Section. A preprint manuscript is under preparation covering system architecture, model performance, and field evaluation.
